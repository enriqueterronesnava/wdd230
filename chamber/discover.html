Discover

It is located in the northern part of the Tamaulipas coast in the Gulf of Mexico, belonging to it most of the Laguna Madre, the largest coastal lagoon in Mexico. A land formed by non-marine marine sediments of the Quaternary, varying its soils in terms of its depth, but being of alluvial origin. It has an important hydrographic feature of great importance that divides it into two. The climate is conditioned precisely by its proximity to the Gulf of Mexico and its continental geographic location. In general, the environment is hot and dry much of the year with regular rains during the summer. Mild in the fall and low temperatures of a few continuous days when the so-called "norths" are presented, which are polar masses loaded with moisture, without snow falling, except in exceptional cases. Frost occurs.
